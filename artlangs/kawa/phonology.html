Kawa has 5 vowels, /i/, /u/, /e/, /o/, /a/. Each occur in both stressed and unstressed syllables. Kawa doesn't have phonemic
length nor phonemic diphthongs, except for one exception; whenever a vowel is followed by a /j/ in the example, imo j teo
/imojteo/ or imojteo /imojteo/.

Phonotactics

Kawa follows the following syllable structure pattern, (C)²V(V)(C)

Initial C
  • All consonants are legal
C²
  • /kn/
  • /gn/
  • /st/
  • /stʷ/
  • /sk/
  • /skʷ/
  • /kʷhʷ/
  • /tʷhʷ/
  • /sʷhʷ/
  • /Nw/*
  • /Nhʷ/
    • [+nasal] > [-voice] /_hʷ
  • /Nj/
    • nj > ɲ

*N refers to all nasals {/m/, /n/, /ŋ/}
V
  • /a/ after all consonants
  • /e/ after all consonants
  • /i/ after all consonants except /j/
  • /o/ after all consonants
  • /u/ after all consonants except /w/ or /hʷ/
Final C
  • /m/, /ŋ/
  • /b/, /t/, /g/
  • /f/, /v/, /s/

Sound changes in Kawa

1) The voiceless labialised velar stop assimilates between vowels and becomes voiced; 2) nasals assimilate before a voiceless labiovelar approximant and become voiceless; 3) an alveolar nasal followed by a palatal approximant assimilates into a palatal nasal and the approximant is dropped; 

    1) kʷ > gʷ /V_V
    2) [+nasal] > [-voice] /_ʍ
    3) nj > ɲ
