Grammar and Syntax

The word order in Kawa is subject-verb-object (SVO). Kawa marks its nouns for case but uses particles to indicate mood and tense for verbs.

Nouns

Nouns decline for 3 cases, the cases are as follows:

  1. Nominative - used when the noun is the subject or the predicate nominative. The thing or person doing the acion: People love me: pesomo mwa imo.
  2. Accusative - used when the noun is the direct object. The thing being acted upon: I love people: imo mwa pesomo.
  3. Genitive - used when the noun is the possessor of or connected with an object: The cat's hair: hawamo gati; the agent of a passive verb: The person was injured by me: o goŋ knot pesomo iŋi; and the partitive:.
  4. Dative - used when the noun is the indirect object or being used in a comparison, certain verbs will also change meaning if the object is dative (tambi to thank, to give thanks to + DAT): I gave them a cat: imo goŋ dewo gato sovia; I am better than you: imo gaŋqe asimus ta.
Note that the nominative and accusative case are identical for all nouns and the distinction is only made in the derogatory pronouns.

Nouns indicate noun case by changing their ending. The ending of the noun depends on whether or not the base-form of the noun ends in a consonant (C) or a vowel (V). They decline as follows:

Nominative / Accusative Genitive Dative
-C -o -i -a
-V -mo -mi -ma

Pronouns

Pronouns declin for case, gender, and derogatress; derogatress is distinction between derogatory pronouns.
