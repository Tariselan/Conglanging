Nouns

Nouns are quite agglutinative. Basic nouns tend to follow this pattern:

Determiners(VC)-Root-Number(CVC)-Case(VC)

Determiners

Nouns use a prefix to indicate the determiner (usually articles). However, if a possive pronoun is used, it is attached before the determiner and the null determiner tends to be used however a speaker can choose to use another article if they would want.

The determiners are as such:

Det. Indefinite Definite Negated Indefinite Negated Definite Null
Affix it- et- id- ed- im-
English Equivelant A The Not a Not the -

Number

Nouns can be singular, dual, or plural.

Singular Dual Plural
-kin- -gin- -zin-

Case

Nouns have 5 distinct grammatical cases: Nominative, vocative, accusative, genitive, and ablative. However, the ablative is functionally both ablative and dative. With ditransitive verbs, accusative is used as the indirect object and the ablative is used as the direct object.

Vocative -ezh
Accusative (IO) -akh
Genitive -iy
