Verbs

Verbs, like nouns, are highly agglutinative. The verbs follow the following pattern:

Voice(V)-Root(C-V)-Subject(CV)-Object(CVC)-Aspect(C)-Mood(VC)-Tense(VC)

Voice

The voice of the verb is either passive or active.

Active Passive
i- e-

Subject

The subject part of the verb encodes both the number and person of the subject. Now this does technically make the verbs somewhat fusional, yes. However you could analyse the subject and object affixes as a person part and a number part.

Singular Dual Plural
1st -ka- -ko- -ku-
2nd -ka- -ko- -ku-
3rd -ma- -mo- -mu-

Object

The object encodes the number and person of the object. It also has a null form for intransitive verbs. If the verb is ditransitive, the object only encodes for the direct object. Again this can still be analysed as number + person + object suffix.

1st 2nd 3rd
Singular -nak- -nok- -nuk-
Dual -kak- -kok- -kuk-
Plural -fak- -fok- -fuk-
Null -mek-

Aspect

The aspect of a verb is either Perfect or Imperfect. Aspect is an odd one out as it is the only affix that doesn't have a vowel.

Perfect Imperfect
-l- -j-

Mood

In this language there are four possible moods: Indicative, Imperative, Subjunctive, Infinitive.

Indicative Imperative Subjunctive Infinitive
-im- -en- -in- -em-

Tense

In the language there are five grammatical tenses; two pasts, present, and two futures. Historical past, recent past, present, near future, far future.

Past Present Future
Historical Recent Near Far
-aw- -ahw- -im- -ow- -ohw-
