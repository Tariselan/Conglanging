Phonotactics:

C(L)V(S)

C → Consonants: /m/, /n/, /ʔ/, /p/, /b/, /t/, /d/, /s/, /ɾ/, /w/, /l/, /j/
L → Liquids: /l/, /ɾ/
V → Vowels: /i(ː)/, /y(ː)/, /u(ː)/, /o(ː)/, /a(ː)/, /e(ː)/
S → Sibilants: /s/

Liquids cannot follow /w/, /l/, /j/, /ɾ/

Stress:

If the word is only 1 syllable long, then it is stressed. If it is 2 or more syllables, the long vowels are stressed.

Word Order:

Adjective → Noun
Verb → Postposition
Verb → Auxiliary
Auxiliary → Postposition
Adverb → Verb

  • VSO
  • Noun-like adjectives
  • The big animal → The big thing the animal → pïwa lixïno
  • pïwa → big (thing)
  • -xu is and but for 2+ subjects and objects
  • Possessee of Possesser
  • -ni → of

Valency Changing Options:

Active→Passive
I see you→You are seen (by me)
see I you→see take you

Active→Causitive
The rock sees the animal→I caused the rock to see the animal
see rock animal→see I cause rock animal
nalaa poyee lixïno→nalaa na lesola poyee lixïno
